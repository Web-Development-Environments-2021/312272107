In the following answer, I will elaborate further on three major differences
between Web 1, 2 and 3.
Firstly, Web 1 was mostly consisted of readers, whilst only few content creators were active.
For exmaple,this website for harvard law
Web 2 has had a different vision in comparison to Web 1
and has focused in social interaction and aspires to encourage users to share
therefore transforming many passive users to become active.
Facebook , 4Chan and TikTok provide great exmaples of that.

Web 3 has taken Web 2 one step further, and has attempted to provide the user with
a personal experience, by focusing on distribution of data and use of sophisticated AI and other tools.
Secondly and to further explain the aforementioned difference, Web 1's focus was on
the dissemination of information, Web 2's objective was to connect different people around the globe
and Web 3's purpose was to provide widespread information to all.
Thirdly, Web 1 was built mostly around companies that cater to consumers' needs
by releasing content they can view.
In stark contrast to Web 1, Web 2 has shifted its focus to people, facillitating
their need to share information.
During the period of Web 2, companies have started to build applications that allow
users to share information with each other; e.g. YouTube, MySpace.
During Web 3, rather than companies, people have begun to construct applications
that make way for users to interact with each other.
Companies have changed their perspective and have begun to manufacture platforms
in which people can offer their unique services and or content.
For example, this site called Tivo which provides a recording program based on personal preferences.
In conclusion, Web 1, 2 and 3 are massively different and so I have presented
three significant differences between them.
